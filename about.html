Background

I've held senior operational leadership roles across technology companies, consulting organizations, and mission-driven institutions — working closely with founders and executive teams to build the systems that support growth, distributed teams, and complex stakeholder environments.

That work has consistently centered on a single problem: the gap between what leadership intends and what the organization actually does. The tools for closing that gap — clear expectations, visible milestones, documented accountability, structured governance — are what I build.

Performance management, to me, is not an HR process. It's a governance tool. The goal is not better annual reviews. It's systems that make accountability consistent, defensible, and durable regardless of who's in the room.

How I Think

Operating Principles

Clarity before evaluation

Expectations must be defined before performance is assessed. Accountability without prior clarity isn't accountability — it's judgment.

Documentation before consequence

Accountability requires shared understanding on record. If it wasn't written down, it didn't happen.

Milestones before judgment

Progress should be visible long before formal reviews. Surprises at review time mean the system failed earlier.

Continuity over personality

Systems should function regardless of who's in the role. If the organization depends on one person's knowledge, it's fragile.
